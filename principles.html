Design Principles

  • Demonstrate value.
  • Build trust through transparency.
  • Create comprehension.
  • Balance form and function.

Demonstrate Value

We know that working in Revinate is not the primary job of our customers.

In order for them to love us, in every interaction our customers should see the value we create for them.

This means we need to know our users deeply, understand what “value” means to them, and bring that value front and center.

Create Comprehension

We make our users feel smart, respect their time, and keep workflows short and meaningful.

We know that they are busy doing many other things.

Our software distills overwhelming data into meaning, simplifies confusing tasks, and brings important information into focus.

Build Trust Through Transparency

Users always know what will happen when they take an action.

Simple and common tasks are frictionless and obvious; complex tasks are efficient and delightful, even when they are interrupted.

Our software allows our users to act with confidence, and trust they can’t make a mistake.

Balance Form and Function

We believe that useful is beautiful, and that good design gets out of the way.

This implies we need to focus on workflow, interaction, and visual design details.

Usability and trust increase when we get the details right.

Brand Voice: Approachable, Concise, Sophisticated

Like a good hotel concierge, we want to:

  • Be approachable, so our customers know they can go to us for help.
  • Respect our customers’ time.
  • Demonstrate an appealing level of sophistication.

Approachable
We meet our customers with empathy, and demonstrate that we understand them.

DO

  • Use consistent terminology across channels (help desk, app, marketing, etc) so that it becomes familiar
  • Use relatable, real examples
  •  

Example: We evaluate competitor data to help you understand your ranking.

DON'T

  • Use overly formal language (unless called for in a particular region)
  • Focus on sales over expertise
  • Use unnecessary jargon or abbreviations (unless they increase clarity)

Example: We utilize comp set to benchmark scorecard data.

Concise
We respect our customers’ time by speaking to them clearly, succinctly, and directly.

DO

  • Use active voice
  • Use actionable, direct language
  • Write just enough to get your point across. Use visual aids to reduce word count.

Example: We have sent you an email to activate your account. If you do not see it, please check your spam folder.

DON'T

  • Use passive voice
  • Make users guess what they need to do
  • Add fluff or flowery language to increase word count

Example: You will have received an email to activate your account. It may have landed in your spam folder.

Sophisticated
We are global citizens and trusted experts in our field.

DO

  • Demonstrate polite language and conventions globally, across touchpoints
  • Share expertise; back up our knowledge with data

Examples:

  • Your guests want a variety of experiences. Here’s how to provide that to them.
  • Usted puede cambiar su contraseña.

DON'T

  • Use sarcasm, overly colloquial language, or pop culture references
  • Use overly informal or “sassy” prose

Examples:

  • Getting your guests to book extra stuff is where the rubber meets the road.
  • Voz puedes cambiar tu contraseña.
